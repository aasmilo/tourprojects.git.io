Places to visit in Albania

Discover the breathtaking beauty and rich cultural heritage of Albania through this virtual tour of its most stunning landmarks.

Berat Castle

Berat, Albania

Berat Castle is a magnificent fortress overlooking the historic town of Berat. Dating mainly from the 13th century, this UNESCO World Heritage site contains numerous Byzantine churches, Ottoman mosques, and traditional houses that offer a glimpse into Albania's rich cultural heritage.

Gjirokastër

Gjirokastër, Albania

Nestled in a valley between the Gjerë mountains and the Drino River, Gjirokastër is a captivating city known as the "City of Stone." Its well-preserved Ottoman architecture, distinctive stone-roofed houses, and imposing castle have earned it recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Butrint National Park

Vlorë County, Albania

Butrint National Park is home to an ancient Greek city and archaeological site that has been occupied since prehistoric times. This UNESCO World Heritage Site features impressive ruins including a theater, temple, and baptistery, all set within a beautiful natural landscape of forests and wetlands.

Krujë Castle

Krujë, Albania

Perched on a mountain overlooking the town of Krujë, this historic castle is one of Albania's most important landmarks. It served as the center of the Albanian resistance against the Ottoman Empire in the 15th century under the leadership of national hero Skanderbeg, whose museum is now housed within the castle walls.

Albanian Riviera

Vlorë County, Albania

The Albanian Riviera is a stunning coastal area along the Ionian Sea, known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters, pristine beaches, and charming Mediterranean villages. From the vibrant nightlife of Dhërmi to the secluded beauty of Gjipe Beach, this stretch of coastline offers something for every traveler.

Lake Ohrid

Border of Albania & North Macedonia

Straddling the mountainous border between southwestern North Macedonia and eastern Albania, Lake Ohrid is one of Europe's deepest and oldest lakes. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its exceptional biodiversity, crystal-clear waters, and numerous historical sites along its shores.

Valbona Valley National Park

Kukës County, Albania

Located in the Albanian Alps, Valbona Valley National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and hikers. The park features rugged mountain peaks, glacial valleys, and the stunning Valbona River. Its pristine beauty and diverse wildlife make it one of Albania's most spectacular natural attractions.
